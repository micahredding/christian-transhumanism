Is the Christian Transhumanist Association a religion or church?

No, the CTA is neither a religion nor a church. It is simply an association for Christians who identify with transhumanism or who recognize transhumanism as a legitimate extension of their faith. We exist to support Christians in their already existing religious commitments, and to enhance those commitments by working to bridge the divide between science and religion.

What are the aims of the association?

To pursue greater unity between science, religion, and technology. To support and enhance the religious commitments of our members. To encourage deeper conversation on the interaction between technology, culture, and our sense of self.

What is the relation between Christianity and Transhumanism?

Increasingly, people are recognizing parallels and complements between Christian and Transhumanist views. On the one hand: Christianity affirms the identity of human beings as made in the image of God, holds out the hope and promise of transformation, and advocates following Jesus Christ forward through faith by grace towards creative and compassionate works.

On the other hand, Transhumanism is a mostly secular ideology that advocates the ethical use of technology to extend human ability, and to improve the human condition.

Despite their apparent differences, Christianity and Transhumanism hold to suprisingly similar views on the nature and potential of human beings, offer resonating visions of transformation, and embrace the shared necessity of working to heal the sick, feed the hungry, and bring life to the dead.
